Principal Investigator

Prof. Erez Shmueli

Erez Shmueli is a Professor at Tel Aviv University and the head of the Big Data Lab. His research spans Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and Healthcare, with a focus on developing methods and algorithms that extract actionable insights from large and complex datasets.

Prof. Shmueli is also a Visiting Professor at Stanford University's Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HAI) Institute, where he collaborates on AI research that keeps people at the center of design and development.

What We Do

Research Areas

Our lab pursues fundamental and applied research across three interconnected pillars.

Artificial Intelligence

Developing novel machine learning and deep learning algorithms that push the boundaries of what AI systems can learn and infer from complex, high-dimensional data.

  • Deep Learning
  • Graph Neural Networks
  • Representation Learning
  • Explainable AI

Data Science

Designing scalable methods for mining patterns, structure, and knowledge from large, heterogeneous datasets — including social networks, mobility traces, and behavioral data.

  • Large-Scale Data Mining
  • Network Science
  • Computational Social Science
  • Privacy & Fairness

Healthcare AI

Applying AI and data science to transform medicine — from predictive modeling of clinical outcomes and electronic health records analysis to population-level epidemiological insights.

  • Clinical Prediction Models
  • EHR Analysis
  • Digital Biomarkers
  • Personalized Medicine
